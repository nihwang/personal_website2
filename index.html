ABOUT ME

My name is Nina and I am a web developer experienced in a variety of languages and frameworks. I graduated from University of California, Davis with a degree in Psychology with Biology emphasis and a minor in Japanese. In March 2014, I graduated from Dev Bootcamp, a 9-week web development program with an emphasis on Ruby on Rails.

So how did a Psychology major become a Web Developer? I started out college as a Pre-Med student and I went about college taking all my science courses. Around my senior year I started to realize that I really hated all my classes. It was time for a change. A friend introduced me to Dev Bootcamp after watching me solve a jigsaw puzzle. He was convinced that my love for jigsaw puzzle would transpire over to coding, and I thought "Hey, can't hurt to try". What I didn't know at the time was how much I was going to fall in love with developing.

I am a creator at heart and I love the idea behind coding. I can simply type into a computer and watch design and figures come to life right in front of my eyes. During DBC I was given the opportunity to lead a team and create one of my own ideas into a web application. There is nothing quite compared to the thrill of watching an idea being made into reality.
